# Bible Study Notes — Systematic Theology Compendium

**Fusion Bible College, Hyderabad**

Two self-contained, offline webpages behind a dashboard landing page (`index.html`):

- **`college_notes.html`** — compiles the college's systematic theology course notes into one searchable, printable study resource
- **`bible.html`** — a full-screen Scripture Presenter for reading and displaying passages during teaching

No internet connection, server, or installation is required — open `index.html` in any modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari) on desktop or mobile and choose which resource to open.

### OT & NT Survey (section 5)

The survey is built from the Bible Panorama wall chart, photographed in 11 panels and arranged in chronological order — Creation → Eden & the Fall → Flood & Babel → Abraham to Moses → Judges & Kings → Captivity & Return → Life of Christ → Cross & Resurrection → Acts → Church History → Eternity. Each panel carries three layers of notes:

- **In the Bible** — the events of that era with scripture references
- **Key notes on this panel** — what to look for in the picture (date anchors, signposts, the book bar, family ribbons)
- **The world at this time** — contemporary history (Sumer, Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Persia, Greece, Rome, the Reformation, the missionary era)

Dating follows the chart's own early-date chronology (e.g. 1446 BC exodus), with the alternative late date noted where relevant.

## How to use

### Navigation
- The left sidebar lists every subject; click a subject name to expand its chapter list and jump directly to any chapter.
- The gold **Q** entry at the bottom of the sidebar jumps to the Question Bank.

### Search
- Type any topic in the top search bar (e.g. *kenosis*, *inerrancy*, *canon*, *atonement*, *Cyrus*, *Tyndale*). All matches are highlighted with a running count; press **Enter** or use the ▲▼ buttons to jump between them.
- Tick **"Matching sections only"** to collapse the entire compendium to just the sections containing your topic — including matching survey panels and question-bank items.
- Any question containing a search match opens automatically so the highlighted answer is visible.

### Question Bank
- Tap a question to reveal its answer, or use **"Show all answers"** in the Question Bank header to open everything at once.
- Use search + "Matching sections only" to pull up all questions on a single topic across every subject.

### Printing
- The **Print** button (or Ctrl+P / Cmd+P) hides all interface chrome and prints clean serif typography, with each subject starting on a fresh page and every survey panel kept with its notes.
- **Print a topic handout:** search a topic, tick "Matching sections only", then print — only the matching sections are printed.
- **Print a test paper:** in the Question Bank, leave answers hidden and print — only questions appear. Show all answers first to print the answer key. Combine with a topic search to print an exam on one subject.

## Updating

The page is generated from the source documents (Word, PowerPoint, Pages, PDF); it is not meant to be edited by hand.

- **Adding Ecclesiology / Eschatology / new notes:** provide the source document and the compendium can be regenerated with the new subject slotted into its numbered position (the greyed sidebar placeholders are already reserved).
- **Correcting content:** correct the source document and regenerate, so the source files remain the single point of truth.
- **Replacing survey photos:** panels 8 and 9 were photographed at an angle around a wall corner; straight-on replacements can be swapped in when available.
